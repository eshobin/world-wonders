The Wonders of the World

Learn more about the world we live in by taking a
look at its wonders and monuments.

Statue of Liberty

New York, US

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal sculpture located
on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. It is a symbol of
freedom and democracy, personified as a woman holding
a torch and a tablet inscribed with the date of the American Declaration of Independence. The statue stands 305 feet
high, including its concrete base, and is made of copper
sheets over a steel framework. Gifted to the United States
by the people of France in 1886, it is a symbol of welcome to immigrants arriving in the United States and a reminder of
the bond between France and the U.S.

Mount Rushmore

South Dakota, US

Mount Rushmore National Memorial is a colossal mountain sculpture in the Black Hills of South Dakota, featuring the
faces of four U.S. presidents: George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Abraham Lincoln, and Theodore Roosevelt. The monument, designed by Gutzon Borglum, aims to
commemorate the first 150 years of American history. The memorial was intended to represent the "birth, growth, development, and preservation" of the United States. It's considered a symbol of American values, freedom,
and democracy.

Gateway Arch

Missouri, US

The Gateway Arch is a 630-foot-tall stainless steel arch
in St. Louis, Missouri, designed by Eero Saarinen. It's
the tallest monument in the United States,
the tallest arch in the world, and a symbol of
westward expansion. It commemorates Thomas
Jefferson, the Louisiana Purchase, and the pioneers
who settled the American West. The arch also stands as
a symbol of American freedom and an architectural triumph.

Golden Gate Bridge

California, US

The Golden Gate Bridge is a suspension bridge that
connects San Francisco, California, to Marin County. It
is a significant landmark, an engineering marvel, and
a major tourist attraction. The bridge is known for its
unique color (international orange), Art Deco detailing,
and the fog that often surrounds it. It spans the
Golden Gate Strait, which connects San Francisco
Bay to the Pacific Ocean.
