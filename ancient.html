Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza was the tomb of pharaoh Khufu, and still contains his granite sarcophagus. Built around 2600 BC, it is a massive structure made of over 2.3 million limestone and granite blocks. It is one of the few ancient wonders that stil exist today.

Hanging Gardens Of Babylon

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon were a massive complex of gardens built on elevated terraces, probably during the reign of King Nebuchadnezzar II in modern-day Iraq. The Gardens would have required incredible engineering and sophisticated irrigation, making them an ancient marvel.

Colosseus Of Rhodes

The Colossus of Rhodes was a gigantic, 110-foot-tall bronze statue of the Greek god Helios (the sun god) that stood in the harbor of Rhodes, Greece, for about 54 years. It was built between 292 and 280 BC and destroyed by an earthquake in 226 BC. It was erected in the city of Rhodes to commemorate a victory over Demetrius Poliorcetes.

Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, a massive tomb built for Mausolus and his sister-wife Artemisia II in Caria (modern-day Turkey). It showcases the architectural and artistic achievements of the Hellenistic period. This tomb was constructed around 350 BCE and it's believed to have stood for over 1,600 years before being damaged by earthquakes.

Temple of Artemis at Ephesus

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus was a grand ancient Greek temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis and is known for its massive size and impressive architecture. Constructed in the 6th century BCE, the temple was initially built of marble and featured 127 Ionic columns, each 60 feet high. It was later rebuilt after being destroyed by fire and by Goths, but ultimately torn down by a Christian mob in 401 CE.

Statue of Zeus at Olympia

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a monumental chryselephantine (gold and ivory) sculpture depicting the god Zeus, seated on a throne. Created by Phidias, it was a magnificent display of classical Greek art and was revered for its beauty and grandeur. In one hand he holds a figure of Nike, the Greek goddess of victory, and in the other he holds a scepter topped with an eagle. His throne features relief sculptures showing many of his children, and his footstool shows a battle between Theseus and the Amazons.
